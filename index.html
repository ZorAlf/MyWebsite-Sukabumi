Sukabumi

Sukabumi City is a city located in the province of West Java, Indonesia. This city is an enclave of Sukabumi Regency.

History

The city of Sukabumi comes from the Sundanese language. That is Suka-Bumen, according to information considering the air is cool and comfortable, those who come to this area do not want to move again because they like / love Bumen-Bumen or reside in this area.

In 1914 the Dutch East Indies Government made Sukabumi City a “Burgerlijk Bestuur” with the status of “Gemeente” on the grounds that there were many Dutch and European plantation owners in the Southern Sukabumi District who had to receive special management and services.

Geography

Sukabumi is the third smallest city (after Cirebon City and Cimahi City) in West Java Province. Sukabumi City with an area of 48.33 km² is located at the foot of Mount Gede and Mount Pangrango at an altitude of 584 meters above sea level.

Despite being located at the foot of a mountain, Sukabumi City is strategically located on the route between the capital of West Java Province and the national capital. Historically, Sukabumi City was built by the Dutch East Indies Government as a Burgerlijk Bestuur (1914) with the status of Gemeenteraad Van Sukabumi which aimed to provide services to the Dutch and Europeans as plantation managers in the Sukabumi, Cianjur and Lebak regencies.

Travel

Situ Gunung Suspension Bridge

Situ Gunung Suspension Bridge is the longest suspension bridge, in the middle of a forest, in Southeast Asia. The bridge is located in Situ Gunung Nature Park, Sukabumi - West Java, which has been a tourist destination for many years and is part of the Gunung Gede Pangrango National Park.

Ciletuh Geopark

Palabuhanratu National Geopark is the only geopark in West Java, Indonesia. Ciletuh-Palabuhanratu National Park is also a new tourist spot in Sukabumi Regency, West Java Province as well as an Indonesian National Geopark that was recognized by UNESCO in 2015 and also recognized by UNESCO Global Geopark UNESCO in 2018.
